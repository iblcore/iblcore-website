**Purpose & Goals**

* **What is the main purpose of the site?**  
  * There are two main purposes:  
    * Serve as a showcase for the new IBL Core to neuroscientists, Research Software Engineers (RSEs), and recruiting partners;  
    * Provide access to our resources, data, tools, and code.  
  * We also want to learn more about our tool users and the demographics of those interested in us.


* **Who are the main audiences?**  
  * The website will mainly target potential future partners and funders, scientists interested in our resources, scientists not yet aware of us, and RSEs exploring career opportunities.


* **What actions do we want visitors to take?**  
* **Understand who we are**   
  * Quickly grasp our mission, values, and unique roles  
  * Action: *Read the “About” section or landing page intro.*  
* **Explore our Work / Services**  
  * Learn what kinds of projects we do and our areas of expertise.  
  * Action: *Click into “Projects” or  “Portfolio”*  
* **Engage / Get in Touch**  
  * Find out how to contact us for partnerships, collaborations, or opportunities.  
  * Action: *Use a contact form, info@ email or go to Slack/Github channel*  
* **Trust-Building**  
  * Reassurance that we are credible and impactful.  
  * Action: *Read “Publications“, “Projects and Partners“ or ~~“Community Impact”~~ page*  
* **Stay Connected**  
  * Keep a relationship alive beyond a single visit.  
  * Action: *Follow us on social media (X, Linkedin, Youtube), subscribe to updates.*  
* **Recruitment / Careers**  
  * Attract engineers, scientists, or collaborators.  
  * Action: *View open positions or join mailing lists.*  
* **Usage tracking**  
  * Tell us if they used data our tools  
  * Action: Answer a form or poll, either option 1 or 2:  
    1. **Lightweight Feedback Form**  
       * A short, friendly form (“Have you used an IBL-Core tool? Tell us how\!”).  
         * Collects: tool name, project type, affiliation, optional feedback.  
       2. **One-Click Poll / Tracker**  
          * A quick button: *“Yes, I’ve used this tool\!”* → logs anonymous stats.  
          * Low-friction, useful for scale metrics.

          ***Note: In-Tool Prompt***

          * *If tools are open-source (GitHub/PyPI/conda), link to the form or tracker from the repo README.*  
          * *Ensures you catch users who never visit your website directly.*

* **How often will content be updated, how, and by whom?**  
  * The website will be updated several times a year by the RSE team and 1–2 admins, by editing Markdown documents in a GitHub repository. The static site will be automatically generated by GitHub Actions and hosted on a service such as Cloudflare Pages.

* **How important is search vs browsing?**  
  * Browsing will be the most important way for users to explore the website, although having a Google search widget will still be useful. In addition, the site may feature a public version of the Marvin AI chatbot to allow users to ask questions directly about us and about our resources.

**Design & Branding**

* **Do we have an existing visual identity** (logo, fonts, colors)?  
  * Yes, although we may want to update it. The key will be to create a clear, distinct identity while still honoring our history. The new logo should draw inspiration from the previous one, but also signal that we are no longer the consortium of 20 labs. Instead, we are now the Core team.  
  * To the graphics designer, it’s important to explain the IBL 2.0 idea: a continuation of IBL 1.0 in spirit and principles, but with a different organizational structure. That shift means our visual identity should evolve as well, keeping continuity with the past while making the new focus unmistakable.
